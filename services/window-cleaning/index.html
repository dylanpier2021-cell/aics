Overview

Clean glass that lets the light back in

Dirty windows are the kind of thing you stop noticing. A little film builds up week by week, the light dims a shade at a time, and then one clean pane reminds you how much brighter a room is supposed to be. AICS handles the full window job, not just a quick pass over the glass.

We clean interior and exterior glass, wipe down the frames, take out the screens and wash them, and clear the sills and tracks where dirt collects, for houses and ground-level storefronts across Urbana, Champaign, Savoy, Mahomet, St. Joseph and Rantoul. No streaks, no missed corners and no film left behind.

What window cleaning includes

Every job is a little different, but a standard window cleaning covers:

  • Interior glass cleaned and polishedEvery inside pane washed and buffed clear so the view is crisp.
  • Exterior glass cleaned and polishedOutside panes washed down and finished streak-free, top to bottom.
  • Screens removed, cleaned and replacedScreens taken out, washed free of dust and pollen, then put back.
  • Sills and tracks wiped free of dirtThe grit and grime that gathers in the channels cleared out by hand.
  • Window frames wiped downFrames and edges wiped so the whole window looks cared for, not just the glass.
  • Hard water spots and buildup treatedMineral spotting and stubborn buildup treated for a clear finish.

Homes and storefronts

For homes, we clean every window inside and out, from the front picture window to the ones behind the kitchen sink. For businesses, we handle storefront and entry glass at ground level, the panes your customers look through and past every day. Recurring plans keep a storefront looking sharp without you having to think about it.

Window cleaning pairs naturally with the rest of our work. Book it alongside commercial cleaning to keep the whole space presentable, or add it to a move-out cleaning so the place shows spotless from the curb to the glass.

How often should you clean your windows?

Most homes do well with a cleaning twice a year, once in spring and once in fall, to clear off the pollen, dust and winter grime. Homes near busy roads, dusty fields or ongoing construction may want to step up to quarterly. Storefronts are usually best on a monthly or quarterly plan, since street-facing glass is what people notice first and it stays presentable when it is cleaned on a schedule.

Good questions

Window cleaning FAQs

How often should windows be cleaned?

Most homes do well with a cleaning twice a year, once in spring and once in fall. If you live near a busy road, a dusty area or an active construction site, quarterly cleaning keeps up better, and busy storefronts often want service monthly or quarterly to stay looking sharp.

Do you clean windows inside and outside?

Yes, we do both. The inside comes up quickly, while the exterior takes more work with ladders and tougher grime, so it makes up the larger share of the cost. Most jobs are quoted for both together.

How much does window cleaning cost?

It commonly runs from about 8 to 40 dollars per window depending on the size, the type and how easy the glass is to reach. A typical whole home lands around 240 to 600 dollars for all the windows cleaned inside and out. We give you an exact price after we know how many windows you have.

Do you clean screens, sills and tracks too?

Yes. Screens are taken out, washed and put back, and the sills and tracks are wiped free of the dirt and grit that collects there. It is all part of the job, not an upsell.

Do you clean commercial and storefront windows?

Yes. We clean ground-level storefront and entry glass for shops and offices, and recurring plans are available to keep your storefront presentable month after month.

Will there be streaks?

No. We use professional tools and technique for a clear, streak-free finish, and we stand behind our work. If something is not right, tell us and we will make it right.
